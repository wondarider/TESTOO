DIGITAL WELLBEING SECURITY FRAMEWORK

The internet is woven into our lives, especially for children and young people. While the online world brings a wealth of benefits, it can also increase the risk of harm or abuse. According to a UNICEF report, one in three people in 30 countries have reported being cyberbullied this problem is snowballing.

And while there are resources available to help teachers and parents become aware of the dangers and some tactics to help address them, there seems to be a gap that draws them together in an easy and reachable manner that easily guides an on how to establish a digital wellbeing program, measure its development and benchmark its performance.

To fill this gap, I am developing the Digital Wellbeing Security Framework - this both illustrates what ‘good protection’ looks like, and provides a structured approach to guide how to get there.

It is also a voluntary toolset that can assess, measure and produce a prioritised uplift roadmap for schools that already have some form of online safety program.

The DWSF is arranged like industry security frameworks, based on the CO:RE 4Cs classification of online risks and is inspired by existing online safety resources..
