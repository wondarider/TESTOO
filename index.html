Helping to keep children safe online through a proven, structured approach to holistic security.

300 million children globably reported being victims of online abuse and sexual exploitation in 2024. A further 300 million reported been subjected to online solicitation, including unwanted sexual talk, un-consensual sexting, unwatned sexual questions or requests for sexual acts (Phys.org, Uni of Edinburgh). .

As a parent and cybersecurity professional, I've seen firsthand how online safety is becoming increasingly complex and concerning to parents and guardians — yet it remains largely overlooked by traditional security companies, leaving educators and parents without structured & embedded guidance on how best to protect the children in their care.

This self funded initiative sets out to address this: by offering a template for establishing a digital wellbeing program and for measuring and continually improving an existing one.
